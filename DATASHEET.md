# Datasheet: INFRADIAN-SYNTH-1K

Following Gebru et al., "Datasheets for Datasets."

## Motivation
Created so the INFRADIAN benchmark can be run end-to-end with **zero data-access friction**: the real
clinical data (mcPHASES) is DUA-restricted, so a permissively-licensed synthetic cohort is what makes
the benchmark reproducible by anyone and makes a distributable reference checkpoint possible.

## Composition
- Synthetic records only. No real people. 600 (default) synthetic participants × 120 days, split
  participant-disjoint into train/validation/test (70/15/15), stratified by cycle regularity.
- One row per (participant, day): relative wearable channels (skin temp deviation, resting HR, RMSSD,
  respiratory rate, sleep efficiency, steps), self-report (menses, cramps, mood, stress), Mira-like
  hormones (E3G, PdG, LH), a 4-phase label, and split/regularity tags.
- ~30% of participants are in the irregular stratum; ~7–18% of cycles (stratum-dependent) are
  anovulatory (no LH surge, no PdG rise, no luteal wearable signature).

## Collection process
Generated by `infradian.synth.generator`, a seeded, deterministic phenomenological model.
Hormone curves are parametric fits to canonical endocrine shapes; wearable coupling uses **verified**
published effect-size ranges (`docs/effect_sizes.md`). A hydration nuisance term is included so the
T1-NC dilution negative control is meaningful.

## Preprocessing / labeling
Phase labels follow the mcPHASES operational geometry. Ground-truth ovulation and anovulation are
generator metadata (real-data ground truth is derived differently, see BENCHMARK.md).

## Uses
Training and evaluating wearable-derived cycle-inference models; hyperparameter tuning for real-data
evaluation with zero selection leakage; teaching. **Not** a substitute for real clinical data and not a
source of clinical facts.

## Distribution & license
CC-BY-4.0, on the HuggingFace Hub as Parquet (dataset-viewer + Croissant compatible).

## Maintenance
Regenerable from source at any seed (`make synth`). Versioned with the repository.

## Limitations
Synthetic. Calibrated to match aggregate effect sizes, not derived from biophysics. Real consumer
wearables are much noisier, that gap is the benchmark's central measurement, not a defect of this data.
